Introduction

Suicide is the third leading cause of death for adolescents, and the second leading cause of death for college students, according to the National Foundation for Suicide Prevention. Mental health - especially in relation to suicide - is considered an uncomfortable topic for most. For this reason and many others, it remains an issue that has not been addressed properly in the past. Because suicide is not talked about more frequently, it is not a widely understood topic. We decided to collect and analyze data surrounding suicide among adolescents to better understand the issue, and provide better insight toward it.

Research Questions

There were three significant questions we wanted to answer through our data:

  1. How do suicide rates compare between male and female adolescents around the world?
  2. How do suicide rates for adolescents in the United States compare to other parts of the world?
  3. How does the rate of human resources for mental health compare to suicide rate for countries around the world?

These three questions can help us understand the causes behind suicide rates in adolescents by comparing factors involved in such rates. Overall, analyzing these data comparisons can help to understand which methods are more effective in benefitting young adultsâ€™ mental health.
